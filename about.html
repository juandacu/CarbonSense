About

We’re six friends from different countries who met during our master’s in climate and energy. We come from economics, engineering, policy, finance, and data—different toolkits, same goal: do our contribution to tackle climate change with clear analysis and practical ideas.

We built Carbon-Sense because the transition is messy and decisions are often made with partial facts. Our backgrounds complement each other: market design and power systems; climate finance and investment; regulation and competition; measurement, reporting, and verification; data and visualization. We test claims against data, show our methods, and keep the writing plain.

We also became close friends through the program. That helps. We argue, we revise, and we keep each other honest. The result is work that’s independent, readable, and useful to people building, regulating, financing, or studying the transition.

What we do

①

Explainers

Plain-English guides to climate and economic concepts, with sources and simple visuals.

②

Applied analysis

Short notes using public datasets and transparent assumptions. When possible, code included.

③

Tools & visuals

Lightweight calculators, maps, and charts to explore trade-offs and sensitivities.

Focus areas

  • Emissions accounting: inventories, scopes, consumption vs production, MRV.
  • Transition finance: flows, instruments, additionality, just-transition costs.
  • Electricity markets: reliability, pricing, renewables integration, policy tests.
  • Jobs & equity: green employment, place-based impacts, distributional effects.
  • Climate risk & regulation: disclosure, stress testing, governance.
  • Methods: reproducible workflows, open data when licensing permits.
